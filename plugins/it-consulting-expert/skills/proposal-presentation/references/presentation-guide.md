# Presentation Format Guide

## Slide Timing Guide

For a 20-minute presentation:

| Section | Slides | Time | Purpose |
|---------|--------|------|---------|
| Title + Agenda | 2 | 1 min | Set context |
| Executive Summary | 1 | 2 min | Hook the audience |
| Client Needs | 2 | 3 min | Build trust — "we understand you" |
| Solution | 3 | 5 min | Core value proposition |
| Approach + Team | 2 | 3 min | Execution credibility |
| Schedule + Cost | 2 | 3 min | Commercial clarity |
| Why Us + Risks | 2 | 2 min | Differentiation and confidence |
| Next Steps | 1 | 1 min | Call to action |
| **Total** | **15** | **20 min** | |

## Presentation Anti-Patterns

**Don't do these:**
- Reading slides verbatim (shows no mastery of content)
- 40+ slides for a 20-minute slot (information overload)
- Generic company introduction taking 5+ minutes (they already know who you are)
- Technical deep-dives without business context (audience is mixed)
- Ignoring evaluation criteria order (evaluators are checking boxes)
- Ending with "Any questions?" instead of a clear next step

## Common Q&A Traps and Responses

**"Your estimate seems high compared to competitors"**
→ Don't defend the number. Ask "Which components seem high?" Then explain the value behind each line item. End with: "We've found that underestimating leads to cost overruns that exceed any initial savings."

**"Can you start next month?"**
→ If you can't: "We can begin requirements analysis next month with our core team. Full team ramp-up would follow in [realistic timeline]. Here's why that sequencing protects project quality..."

**"What if your key person leaves?"**
→ "We assign a shadow for every critical role from day one. Knowledge is in our methodology and documentation, not just in individuals. Here's our specific continuity plan..."

**"Why not use [cheaper technology]?"**
→ Never disparage the alternative. Instead: "Both are viable. We recommended [X] because it specifically addresses your [requirement from RFP]. The total cost of ownership over 3 years actually favors [X] because..."
